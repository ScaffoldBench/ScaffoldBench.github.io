ScaffoldBench: A Dynamic Benchmark for Evaluating Pedagogical Scaffolding and ZPD Alignment in Large Language Models

Anonymous Submission
For ACL 2026 Blind Review Only
Code & Dataset

ScaffoldBench shifts evaluation from result-oriented accuracy to pedagogical fidelity. While standard LLMs prioritize answer correctness (often leaking the solution), ScaffoldBench evaluates the ability to diagnose the Zone of Proximal Development (ZPD) and provide Socratic guidance.

Abstract

While Large Language Models (LLMs) show significant potential in education, existing evaluation paradigms prioritize result accuracy and static safety, often overlooking the dynamic capacity for pedagogical scaffolding grounded in the Zone of Proximal Development (ZPD).

To address this deficiency, we present ScaffoldBench, a dynamic framework for evaluating alignment between model interventions and latent student cognitive states. We utilize a controllable simulation pipeline to generate high-fidelity student interaction traces based on deterministic cognitive profiles, establishing ground-truth labels for diagnostic assessment.

Our comprehensive evaluation of 17 models across Physics and Reading domains reveals a critical trade-off between reasoning capability and scaffolding effectiveness. Specifically, we identify a "Reasoning Trap": reasoning-enhanced models excel at diagnosis but fail to inhibit answer leakage, while non-reasoning models struggle with pedagogical adherence under explicit objectives. These findings demonstrate that effective pedagogical guidance requires precise diagnostic mechanisms beyond generic reasoning capabilities.

Methodology & Key Findings

Data Construction Pipeline: A three-stage process including Pedagogical Graph Construction, ZPD-Conditioned Student Simulation, and Human-in-the-loop Validation.

The "Reasoning Trap": Elevated Answer Leakage Rate (ALR) reveals the conflict between CoT mechanisms and pedagogical inhibition.

Qualitative Analysis: The Oracle setting enables a shift from generic correction to constructivist, validate-and-differentiate strategies.

Cognitive Morphologies: Distribution of interaction trajectories across Physics (MMLU) and English (RACE) domains.
